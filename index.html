            Heart failure is a type of cardiovascular disease in which the heart muscle is  

            either too weak or too stiff to efficiently pump blood, resulting in fluid buildup,

            shortness of breath, and other difficulties. The most commonly used 

            categorization system for determining the severity of a patient's symptoms is 

            the New York Heart Association (NYHA) Functional categorisation system. 



            The NYHA system categorises patients into one of four groups based on theirbr  

            physical activity limits, with Class I being the least severe and Class IV being the 

            most severe. Ejection fraction is also measured by clinicians to evaluate how 

            efficiently the heart pumps blood.
